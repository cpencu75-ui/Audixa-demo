Before you start

What you are testing — and why it matters

Read this first. It takes 90 seconds and makes everything else make sense.

Audixa is not a music generator. Suno, Udio, and Minimax generate music. Audixa generates the precise text instructions — called prompts — that tell those platforms exactly what to create.

Without a good prompt, Suno produces generic results. With an Audixa-engineered prompt, you get music theory, psychoacoustics, mixing vocabulary, and platform-specific tags — all packed into 180 characters automatically.

Your job as a tester is simple: use the app naturally, notice what surprises you, and tell us what you think. There are no wrong answers.

This is a Demo version. The output is simulated by a local engine — no internet connection required for generation. The real version connects to Claude, GPT-4o, or Gemini. The prompt quality is representative of real AI output.

Step 3 of 5

Generate your first prompt

Three clicks in Easy Mode. Then explore deeper if you want.

The app has three experience levels. Start with Easy Mode — it is the green button in the level bar below the tabs.

  • 1
    Make sure Easy Mode is selected

    Look for the level bar: ✨ Easy · 🎸 Creator · 🎯 Pro. Click ✨ Easy — it should turn green.

  • 2
    Choose a vibe card

    Eight cards appear: Epic, Dreamy, Dark, Chill, Groovy, Emotional, Raw, Ambient. Click any one. Think about what kind of music you want — for a student film, a podcast intro, a reel, or just something you would enjoy listening to.

  • 3
    Set the energy level

    Move the slider from Whisper to Explosive. This controls BPM range, dynamics, and arrangement density. Leave it at Medium if unsure.

  • 4
    Press Generate

    The button is the large purple gradient button at the bottom. The app will take 1–2 seconds to generate (simulating a real AI response time). Three prompt cards appear.

In the demo version, output is generated by a local simulation engine — not a live AI. This means the engine gives you representative, high-quality prompt examples based on your selections, but they are deterministic rather than creatively unique each time. The real product uses Claude, GPT-4o, or Gemini.

Step 4 of 5

Open the prompt in your music platform

One click sends the prompt directly to Suno, Udio, or whichever platform you selected.

  • 1
    Click "Open in Suno" (or "Open in Udio / Minimax")

    The button label matches the platform you chose in Step 2. Clicking it opens that platform in a new browser tab with your prompt pre-filled in the style field wherever the platform's URL supports it.

  • 2
    If you do not have an account on that platform — create one free

    Suno: go to suno.com → Sign up with Google. Free tier gives you 10 generations per day.
    Udio: go to udio.com → Sign up. Free credits available.
    Minimax: go to hailuomusic.com → Register. Free credits on signup.

  • 3
    Paste the prompt if it did not auto-fill

    Click Copy on the Audixa card, then paste into Suno's Style field. For Suno: the Style field is the second text area on the Create page — not the Lyrics field.

  • 4
    Generate 2 variations and listen

    In Suno, click Create twice to generate two versions. Listen to both. Apply the quick curation test: does it hook you in the first 30 seconds? Is there a moment that surprises you?

You do not need a paid Suno subscription to test this. The free tier is sufficient. The purpose is to hear how Audixa-generated prompts perform compared to typing a description yourself.
Optional — explore further

The other five studios

Each tab is a different creative tool. Try at least one more after Easy Mode.

  • ♪
    Lyric Architect

    Click the Lyric Architect tab. Write a theme or concept (e.g. "a late-night drive, feeling free but lost"). Choose a song structure and press Generate. You receive: full lyrics with Suno structural tags already formatted, plus a separate Style Prompt to paste into Suno's Style field. Copy each to the correct field.

  • →
    Extension Engine

    Have a Suno track you want to continue? Paste your original prompt, choose an extension type (Build / Climax / Cool Down), and generate continuation prompts for Suno's Extend feature.

  • 📈
    Catalog Studio

    The commercial production hub. Select a market niche (Corporate Uplifting, Lo-Fi Hip-Hop, Meditation, etc.), set the quantity to 5, press Generate Catalog Prompts. You receive 5 commercial prompts — each with a quality score and a "commercial hook" describing exactly what content each track is perfect for.

    After generating, click Generate Metadata to see a title, description, 15 SEO tags, and Pond5/AudioJungle pricing for the first track.

  • 🎬
    Sync Studio (in Catalog Studio tab, scroll down)

    Enter a track title, mood, and ideal placement (TV drama, game cutscene, ad). Press Generate Sync Pitch Package. You receive a one-liner pitch, professional description, sync tags, and a cold email template for pitching to music supervisors.

You do not need to try every studio. The most important thing is to reach at least one result in Suno (or another platform) and evaluate the quality honestly. Everything else is bonus exploration.

Quick reference

Common questions

  • ?
    The prompt is in English but I want to generate in another language

    Suno, Udio, and Minimax all understand English style prompts regardless of the output language. In Lyric Architect, switch the Language selector to generate lyrics in Spanish, French, Portuguese, German, Italian, Japanese, or Korean.

  • ?
    The "Open in Suno" button opened the site but the prompt was not there

    Some browsers block URL parameters. Click Copy on the card, go to suno.com/create, and paste manually into the Style field (the second text input on the Create page).

  • ?
    The output quality is different each time I click Generate

    In the demo, output is deterministic — the same inputs produce the same outputs. Variation comes from your choices (vibe, energy, genre). In the real version with a live AI, every generation is uniquely creative.

  • ?
    Can I use the music I generate for my student project?

    Yes. Suno's free tier allows personal and educational use. For projects you intend to publish commercially (YouTube monetization, film festivals, etc.), you need Suno Pro ($10/month), which grants full commercial use rights.

  • ?
    Is this going to replace music production as a career?

    No. AI generates sound. The human decisions — which vibe, which platform, which variation to keep, how to use the music — are the creative act. Audixa is a tool for that creative direction, not a replacement for musical judgment. The producers who succeed with AI are those with the best curation instinct — exactly what a music education develops.
